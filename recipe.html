Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter) or unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup milk (adjust as needed)
  • A pinch of salt

For the sugar syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 2-3 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • A few drops of rose water (optional)

For Frying:

  • Oil or ghee (for deep frying)

Recipe

Make the Sugar Syrup:

  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water.
  2. Add crushed cardamom pods.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Add rose water (if using) and set aside to cool.

Prepare the Dough:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, baking soda, ghee, and salt.
  2. Gradually add milk and knead gently until you form a smooth dough. Dont over-knead; it should be soft and pliable.

Shape the Balls:

  1. Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls (about the size of a marble). Make sure there are no cracks.

Fry the Gulab Jamun:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan over low to medium heat.
  2. Once hot, carefully drop the balls into the oil.
  3. Fry until they turn golden brown, turning occasionally to cook evenly. This can take about 7-10 minutes.

Soak in Syrup:

  1. Remove the fried gulab jamuns and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  2. Once slightly cooled, transfer them to the warm sugar syrup.
  3. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours (they'll expand and absorb the syrup).

Serve:

Enjoy your homemade Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature! They are delicious as a dessert or a sweet treat.

Barfi

Ingredients

  • Condensed Milk - 1 can
  • Milk Powder - 2 cups
  • Ghee - 2-3 tablespoons
  • Nuts (like almonds, cashews, or pistachios) - chopped, about 1/2 cup
  • Cardamom Powder - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Sugar - optional, depending on your sweetness

Recipe

Prepare the Pan:

  1. Grease a square or rectangular pan with ghee and set aside.

Cook the Mixture:

  1. In a non-stick pan, heat the ghee over medium heat.
  2. Add the milk powder and mix well.
  3. Pour in the condensed milk and stir continuously to avoid lumps.
  4. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the mixture thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan.

Add Flavorings:

  1. Add the chopped nuts, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Mix well.

Set the Mixture:

  1. Once the mixture is well combined and thick, remove it from the heat.
  2. Pour the mixture into the greased pan and spread it evenly.
  3. Press it down firmly with a spatula.

Garnish:

  1. Sprinkle some chopped nuts on top for garnish, pressing them lightly into the mixture.

Cool and Cut:

  1. Allow it to cool at room temperature for a few hours. Once set, cut it into squares or diamonds.

Serve:

Enjoy your homemade Barfi! Store it in an airtight container.
